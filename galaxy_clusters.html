Galaxy Clusters

A galaxy cluster or cluster of galaxies, is a structure that consists anywhere from hundreds to thousands of galaxies that are bound together by gravity.

They are the largest known gravitationally bound structures in the universe and were believed to be the largest known structures in the universe until the 1980s, when superclusters were discovered.

One of the key features of clusters is the intracluster medium (ICM).
