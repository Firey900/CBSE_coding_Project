Open Clusters

An open cluster is a type of star cluster made of up to a few thousand stars that were formed from the same giant molecular cloud and have roughly the same age.

More than 1,100 open clusters have been discovered within the Milky Way Galaxy, and many more are thought to exist.

They are loosely bound by mutual gravitational attraction and become disrupted by close encounters with other clusters and clouds of gas as they orbit the galactic center.
