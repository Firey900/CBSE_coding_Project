Planets

A planet is an astronomical body orbiting a star or stellar remnant that is massive enough to be rounded by its own gravity.

The term planet is ancient, with ties to history, astrology, science, mythology and religion.

Planets in the Solar System are divided into two main types: large low-density giant planets, and smaller rocky terrestrials.

In order of increasing distance from the Sun, they are the four terrestrials planets, Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, then the four giant planets, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Six of the planets are orbited by one or more natural satellites, the two exceptions being Mercury and Venus.
